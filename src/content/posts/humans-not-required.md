# AI is good

To deny that AI has tons of benefit and has been a really a helpful tool is to deny that sugar is sweet.

Cancer research just got a shot in the arm because AI can actually read medical images and find tumors faster than some radiologists. Protein folding, drug discovery, real-time translation breaking down language barriers, these are not nothing. For a blind person, AI describing the world through a camera is basically a superpower. There's genuine good here, and pretending otherwise is just as dumb as the people who think AI can do no wrong.

The problem is that the good stuff gets used as cover for the bad stuff. Like a drug dealer who also donates to orphanages.

# AI is cancer

To deny that AI does more damage to society and humanity as a whole is to deny that sugar can be bad for your health when you ingest lots of it.

Let's start with the obvious: training a single large model emits as much carbon as five cars over their entire lifetimes. And for what? So Silicon Valley can charge you $20 a month to write emails you could have written yourself.

Then there's the data. Every prompt you type, every image you generate, every conversation, it's all scraped, stored, and fed back into the machine. You're not the customer, you're the raw material. And the people running these models? They're building a world where creativity becomes a prompt, where art becomes a pattern match, where the entire web gets flooded with sludge until nobody can tell what's real anymore.

And the real killer, it's concentrating power in ways we don't even have language for yet. A handful of companies decide what the model says, what it won't say, what truth looks like. That's not innovation. That's a new kind of infrastructure, and we're all just living on top of it.

# The Human factor

They say use those who use AI will replace those who dont, but dont we see who retarted that sounds? Ai can be a tool, but AI is not just a tool, it can't reason, but it seems to learn. And eventually those who use AI became trainining data.

Here's the thing nobody wants to say out loud: every time you reach for AI to write, think, or decide for you, you're outsourcing a piece of yourself. You get the answer faster, but you lose the muscle. The ability to struggle through a problem, to be wrong, to arrive at something messy and yours, that atrophies. And what grows in its place is dependence.

They frame it as a productivity hack. But productivity for what? To produce more for who? You're not climbing a ladder, you're on a treadmill, and the speed keeps going up. Meanwhile every thought you type into that box becomes a data point that trains the thing that will eventually do your job for free. You're literally teaching your replacement.

And the cruelest part? The people selling you this future don't use it the same way. They send their kids to Waldorf schools, no screens, no AI. They know.

# AI, AI, Go away

Love it or hate it, it's already here. there's no no-Ai anymore. US companies cant stop working on the technology even if they are aware of the inpending doom because, _hell, the chinese wont stop so if we do stop, we're just gonna be left behind_.

That's the trap, isn't it? We've turned a technological choice into an arms race. No company can afford to sit out because their competitor won't. No country can afford to pause because the other won't. So we all just keep sprinting toward a cliff together, each one afraid to be the first to stop.

And the rest of us? We're just caught in the middle. We don't get a vote. We don't get opt-out. AI is being deployed into our workplaces, our schools, our governments, our hospitals, whether we want it or not. The decision was made for us by people who stand to profit from it.

You can't opt out of a system that's being built around you. You can only watch it go up.

# The Future<span style="color: red;">.</span>

I don't have a hopeful ending for you. I don't have a plan or a call to action. Though that doesn't mean the future is grim or this post is meant to come of as a dooms day warning. The cat is already out of the bag, the machine is already running, and none of us were asked if we wanted it turned on. And to be honest there's no need crying over spilled milk, if there's a cat in the house. We do need AI, as a tool for us to do better, to evolve in ways every other technological advancement has help us evolve in this past. Not as the thing that replaces our value in society, economy, art...

Maybe we regulate. Maybe we adapt. But one thing's for sure, we will all wake up one day and realize we handed over the one thing that made us human.. the need to try, to fail, to be imperfect, to reason, to figure it out ourselves.

Or maybe we just keep feeding it until there's nothing left to feed. maybe then we'll realize that the future is coming, and we're not required.
